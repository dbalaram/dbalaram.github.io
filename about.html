About Me

Linear algebra in grad school wasn’t just math—it was a turning point. I still remember the moment it all clicked while trying to visualize the Jacobian. It was like discovering a hidden framework that made sense of everything. Suddenly, matrices and transformations became more than abstract ideas—they were the building blocks for solving real-world problems. What drives me now is taking that abstract math and making it work in practical, meaningful ways. From designing models that reshape industries to helping creatives push boundaries, I thrive on finding elegant solutions to complex challenges. It’s not magic, but there’s something endlessly satisfying about turning mathematical chaos into innovation that matters.
